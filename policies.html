Working together

Working Together to Respect Eachother - Our Policy and Procedures

Anti-bullying policy: APU Club defines bullying as one of the five categories of abuse. Having an Anti-bullying policy in place will help everyone understand what is and is not acceptable within your club. Having a clear process will help your club to manage any issues and hopefully prevent bullying.


Eligibility Requirements: Players must maintain a certain academic standard to remain eligible to participate in the football club. This could include a minimum GPA or regular attendance in classes. Academic Support: Offering tutoring or study sessions to ensure that players balance their academic responsibilities with their athletic commitments.


Non-Discrimination Policy: The club should be open to players of all backgrounds, regardless of race, gender, or sexual orientation. Discriminatory behavior should be strictly prohibited. Inclusive Practices: Promoting inclusivity by encouraging participation from all segments of the university community and ensuring a welcoming environment.


Zero Tolerance for Drugs and Alcohol: Strictly prohibiting the use of drugs and alcohol during team activities, with clear consequences for violations. Education Programs: Regular workshops on the dangers of substance abuse and the importance of maintaining a healthy lifestyle.


Budget Management: Clear policies on the management of funds, including how money is raised, allocated, and spent. Regular audits should be conducted to ensure transparency. Fundraising Activities: Guidelines for conducting fundraising activities, ensuring that all efforts are ethical and aligned with the university's values.


Service Projects: Encouraging players to participate in community service projects, enhancing the club's reputation and fostering a sense of social responsibility. Public Relations: Policies for interacting with the media and the public, ensuring that the club's image is maintained positively.


Sustainability Practices: Encouraging the use of sustainable practices in club activities, such as reducing waste and promoting recycling during events and games.
